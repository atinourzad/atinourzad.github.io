About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in Electrical & Computer Engineering at The Ohio State University, advised by Prof. Ness B. Shroff.

My research sits at the intersection of Machine Learning (ML) and Natural Language Processing (NLP). I think of myself as a "full-stack" researcher: I develop novel algorithms for LLM alignment (RLxF), multi-objective optimization, and AI Safety, and I build the scalable distributed systems required to train and evaluate them in real-world settings.

My work has resulted in multiple first-author publications, including contributions to top-tier venues like ICML 2025.

Featured Projects

COMPASS Algorithms
An advanced RLxF (GRPO) algorithm to generate preference-conditioned LLM responses.
Impact: Addressed reward hacking and validated robustness using Pareto-front analysis and external LLM judges.
FIRM Privacy & Theory
A communication-efficient framework for multi-objective alignment in distributed settings.
Impact: Provided the first finite-time convergence guarantees and mitigated client "disagreement drift."
FLAGON Systems
A scalable, federated LLM pre- and post-training system (Llama-3 1B) using AWS coordinators.
Impact: Cut cross-site traffic by 75% and built a fault-tolerant pipeline for geo-distributed clients.
